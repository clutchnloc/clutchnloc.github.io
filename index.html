The problem this solves

Clients coming overdue on purpose. Stylists pulling numbers from thin air. The ClutchNLoc Overdue Retie Formula creates a calculated baseline — a minimum charge that is fair to both the loctician and the client — so pricing is never a guessing game.

The formula

ClutchNLoc Overdue Retie Formula
Baseline = (Weeks Overdue ÷ Standard Interval) × Current Retie Price
This is the minimum charge. Adjust based on effort at consultation.

How to use the baseline

Went faster / less effort than expected
Give the client a break — charge below the baseline. Your call, your discretion.
Effort matched expectation
Charge what the formula calculated. No guesswork, no awkward conversations.
More effort than calculated
The baseline is the floor — add on charges for extra work.

Pro tips for overdue reties

01 Always do a consultation first — never sit a client down and start without assessing the work.
02 At consultation, retie approximately 5 locs and time yourself. This tells you the actual pace before committing to the full service.
03 New client who has never been to you? From experience and research: 1 pull-through ≈ 1 week of growth. Use this to estimate their overdue status.
04 Fixed rate pricing is required for this formula to work as intended. Variable pricing complicates the baseline calculation.
Suggested additions
05 Collect a non-refundable deposit for overdue clients when booking. It protects your time and filters out clients who are not serious.
06 Create a written overdue policy and send it with your booking confirmation. No surprises at the chair means smoother transactions.

Why this formula works

The formula ties your overdue charge directly to your own pricing — so it scales automatically when you raise your rates. It is defensible, explainable, and fair. Clients who were strategically coming overdue to save money no longer have a financial incentive to do so, because the formula closes that loophole. And because it is a minimum, not a fixed fee, you keep professional discretion over the final number.
